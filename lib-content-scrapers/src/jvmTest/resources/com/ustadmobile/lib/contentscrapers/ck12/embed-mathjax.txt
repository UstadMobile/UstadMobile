<h3 id="x-ck12-R3VpZGFuY2U."><span>Midpoints and Segment Bisectors</span></h3>
<p id="x-ck12-YjQ4ZTQ3YmFjZmRjMGNmZjU0NWI4YzQ1MDVhYjA2ODk.-b7p">When two segments are congruent, we indicate that they are congruent, or of equal length, with <span class="x-ck12-underline">segment markings</span>, as shown below:</p>
<p id="x-ck12-OThlZDYwZGIxYzVmMTMzNTlmYjYxMDYwYTljOThlNDk.-sde"><span class="x-ck12-img-inline"><!-- @@author="" --><!-- @@license="" --><img data-flx-url="/flx/show/image/201502261424993995157108_588ff8036484739364493a03e596e988-201502261424996108674496.png" src="/media/test2picture.png" width="320" alt="" /></span></p>
<p id="x-ck12-NWZlYmFkOTUyNWQ4ODBmYjU0YmQ0OWJiYjRmZmZjZTA.-czn">A <strong>midpoint</strong> is a point on a line segment that divides it into two congruent segments.</p>
<p id="x-ck12-OThlZDYwZGIxYzVmMTMzNTlmYjYxMDYwYTljOThlNDk.-hcq"><span class="x-ck12-img-inline"><!-- @@author="" --><!-- @@license="" --><img data-flx-url="/flx/show/image/201502261424993995174918_f7544e7ededc28002f16e3f336e86e66-201502261424996108893779.png" src="/media/test1picture.png" width="150" alt="" /></span></p>
<p id="x-ck12-MzlmMzU2MTcxZjVjM2Y1YTEwZDE4NTA0N2EzMDEzYTc.-nsk">Because <span class="x-ck12-mathEditor" data-mathmethod="inline" data-contenteditable="false" data-edithtml="" data-tex="AB%20%3D%20BC%2C%20%5C%20B" data-math-class="x-ck12-math"></span> is the midpoint of <span class="x-ck12-mathEditor" data-mathmethod="inline" data-contenteditable="false" data-edithtml="" data-tex="%5Coverline%7BAC%7D" data-math-class="x-ck12-math"></span>. Any line segment will have exactly one midpoint.</p>
<p id="x-ck12-MmVjYjAyYmYyNmM0NzBkYzI1NmYwOTAyYzU0YWEzMTI.-nmc">When points are plotted in the coordinate plane, we can use a formula to find the midpoint between them.</p>
<p id="x-ck12-NTI3ZjNiZjRmY2E4M2U3MDVhMGY5OTUwZGEzNjFlOWE.-y6h">Here are two points, (-5, 6) and (3, 2).</p>
<p id="x-ck12-OThlZDYwZGIxYzVmMTMzNTlmYjYxMDYwYTljOThlNDk.-egd"><span class="x-ck12-img-inline"><!-- @@author="" --><!-- @@license="" --><img data-flx-url="/flx/show/image/201502261424993995250018_d35bc7189e3b7b2c80be76902e7d6bd3-201502261424996109071515.png" src="/media/test3picture.png" width="250" alt="" /></span></p>
<p id="x-ck12-MzliMjAzNTYwYjNjN2E2YzkwMTI5MTFhYmMxYWY5N2U.-hr2">The midpoint should be halfway between the points on the segment connecting them. Just by looking, it seems like the midpoint is (-1, 4).</p>
<p id="x-ck12-ODU5YWNhZTYxMmNmY2I3MTVmYmU0NTY3NDdlODZhNWI.-5xd"><strong>Midpoint Formula:</strong> For two points, <span class="x-ck12-mathEditor" data-mathmethod="inline" data-contenteditable="false" data-edithtml="" data-tex="%28x_1%2C%20y_1%29" data-math-class="x-ck12-math"></span> and <span class="x-ck12-mathEditor" data-mathmethod="inline" data-contenteditable="false" data-edithtml="" data-tex="x_2%2C%20y_2" data-math-class="x-ck12-math"></span>, the midpoint is <span class="x-ck12-mathEditor" data-mathmethod="inline" data-contenteditable="false" data-edithtml="" data-tex="%5Cleft%20%28%20%5Cfrac%7Bx_1%2Bx_2%7D%7B2%7D%2C%20%5C%20%5Cfrac%7By_1%2By_2%7D%7B2%7D%20%5Cright%20%29" data-math-class="x-ck12-math"></span>.</p>
<p id="x-ck12-Mzk2ZGE3NjNlOGYzODFlZDM0Y2JkMWQ0M2JhYzkzM2I.-ars">Letâ€™s use the formula to make sure (-1, 4) is the midpoint between (-5, 6) and (3, 2).</p>
<p id="x-ck12-6zw"><span class="x-ck12-mathEditor" data-mathmethod="block" data-contenteditable="false" data-edithtml="" data-tex="%5Cleft%20%28%20%5Cfrac%7B-5%2B3%7D%7B2%7D%2C%20%5C%20%5Cfrac%7B6%2B2%7D%7B2%7D%20%5Cright%20%29%20%3D%20%5Cleft%20%28%5Cfrac%7B-2%7D%7B2%7D%2C%20%5Cfrac%7B8%7D%7B2%7D%20%5Cright%20%29%20%3D%20%28-1%2C%204%29" data-math-class="x-ck12-block-math"></span></p>
<p id="x-ck12-MTllNGVlZGVkMzFiZThmMDQ4ZGFmYWM2NGM1MTJiNDQ.-srn">A <strong>segment bisector</strong> cuts a line segment into two congruent parts and passes through the midpoint. A <strong>perpendicular bisector</strong> is a segment bisector that intersects the segment at a right angle.</p>
<p id="x-ck12-lli"><span class="x-ck12-mathEditor" data-mathmethod="block" data-contenteditable="false" data-edithtml="" data-tex="%26%5Coverline%7BAB%7D%20%5Ccong%20%5Coverline%7BBC%7D%5C%5C%0A%26%5Coverline%7BAC%7D%20%5Cperp%20%5Coverleftrightarrow%7BDE%7D" data-math-class="x-ck12-block-math"></span></p>
